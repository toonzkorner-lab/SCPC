Texas heat is notorious for taking a toll on exterior building materials. Fortunately, high-quality architectural precast concrete and GFRC (Glass Fiber Reinforced Concrete) are incredibly resilient options for outdoor features like columns, fire pits, wall caps, and planters. 

While precast concrete is built to last, routine maintenance ensures your installations look pristine for decades, despite brutal UV exposure and intense thermal expansion.

## 1. Understanding Thermal Expansion

In regions like San Antonio, temperature swings between blistering summer afternoons and cool nights can cause materials to expand and contract. SCPC Precast engineers our products with these tolerances in mind. 

However, the **joints** between precast segments are where maintenance is critical. 
- Inspect the caulking and sealants around column joints and wall caps annually. 
- If you notice cracking or shrinking in the sealant, it’s time to re-caulk with a high-grade, UV-resistant polyurethane sealant to prevent moisture ingress during sudden storms.

## 2. Sealing Against UV and Moisture

Precast concrete is porous. To protect the finish from fading under the Texas sun and to prevent water absorption, a high-quality concrete sealer is highly recommended.

- **Penetrating Sealers:** For a natural look, use a silane-siloxane penetrating sealer. It bonds within the concrete's pores, offering excellent protection against water and UV degradation without altering the texture or color.
- **Application Frequency:** Reapply penetrating sealers every 3 to 5 years, depending on direct sun exposure.

## 3. Cleaning Dust, Dirt, and Organic Growth

The dry, dusty Texas environment, interspersed with occasional humidity, can lead to dirt buildup or mildew in shaded areas.

- **Routine Cleaning:** Wash your precast elements with a gentle spray from a garden hose. 
- **Deep Cleaning:** If dirt is caked on, use a soft-bristle brush with a mild, non-acidic detergent (like a neutral pH stone cleaner).
- **Avoid Pressure Washing:** High-pressure washing can etch the surface of the concrete, removing the protective "cream" layer and exposing aggregates. If you must pressure wash, keep it under 1500 PSI and use a wide-fan tip from a safe distance.

## 4. Protecting GFRC Installations

GFRC elements (like lightweight planters and complex moldings) contain glass fibers instead of steel reinforcement, making them immune to internal rust or "spalling." This makes GFRC exceptionally durable in harsh climates. 

However, GFRC elements often feature highly detailed finishes. Maintain these details by avoiding harsh chemical cleaners (like muriatic acid) which can degrade the fine surface texture.
